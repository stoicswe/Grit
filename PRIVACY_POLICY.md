# Privacy Policy

**Grit for GitLab**
*Last updated: April 12, 2026*

## Overview

Grit is a GitLab client for iOS. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data Grit does — and does not — collect.

**In short: Grit does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. All information stays on your device.**

## Data Collection

Grit does **not** collect any user data. The app does not include any analytics frameworks, tracking SDKs, advertising identifiers, or telemetry of any kind.

- **No analytics or telemetry** — Grit does not track app usage, screen views, taps, or user behavior.
- **No crash reporting** — Grit does not send crash reports to any third-party service.
- **No advertising** — Grit contains no ads and does not use advertising identifiers.
- **No cross-app or cross-site tracking** — Grit does not participate in any form of tracking across apps or websites.

## Data Stored on Your Device

Grit stores the following information **locally on your device only**:

- **Authentication credentials** — Your GitLab access token or OAuth session is stored securely in the iOS Keychain. It is never transmitted anywhere other than your configured GitLab instance.
- **App preferences** — Settings such as appearance, accent color, and notification preferences are stored in UserDefaults on your device.
- **Cached API responses** — Lightweight caches of GitLab API data are stored locally to improve performance. This data is never sent to any third party.

None of this data ever leaves your device except to communicate directly with the GitLab instance you configure.

## Voluntary Issue Reporting

Grit includes an optional **Report an Issue** feature in Settings. If you choose to use it, the app will:

1. Collect basic device information (app version, device model, iOS version).
2. Gather app log entries from the last 60 minutes. These logs contain only technical events such as network requests and navigation — **never** your GitLab tokens, passwords, or personal data. Only logs from the Grit app process are collected; no system logs or data from other apps is included.
3. Open your device's mail app with a pre-filled email and the diagnostic log attached.

**You have full control over this process.** You can review, edit, or discard the email before sending. No data is sent automatically — it is only submitted if you explicitly choose to send the email. The report is sent directly to the developer at `contact@stoicswe.com` via your own email client.

## Third-Party Services

Grit communicates exclusively with the GitLab instance you configure (e.g., `gitlab.com` or your self-hosted server). No data is shared with any other third-party service.

## Children's Privacy

Grit does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13.

## Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is updated, the revised version will be posted here with an updated date. Since Grit collects no data, meaningful changes are unlikely.
